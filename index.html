Departure & Karoo

The Great Karoo — Northern Cape
Day 01Mon 27 Jan~750 km · 7 hrs
Johannesburg → Matjiesfontein

Hit the N1 before dawn. The Highveld gives way to the vast, alien Karoo by mid-morning — a landscape of almost overwhelming silence and scale. Overnight in Matjiesfontein, a perfectly preserved Victorian ghost town that exists in suspended time, with zero light pollution and one of the great starscapes on the continent.

What to do
★ Landmark
Three Sisters Rocks
Iconic inselberg trio rising from the plains — great roadside stop
☸ Culture
Red Bus Town Tour
Double-decker bus tour of Matjiesfontein with costumed guide
⎈ Architecture
Victorian Ghost Town Walk
Explore the 1800s railway town — nothing has changed since
📷 Photography
Milky Way Session
Zero light pollution — one of SA's best astrophotography spots
🌞 Sunset
Hotel Veranda Sundowner
Lord Milner veranda, cold beer, orange Karoo light
■ Photographer's Note

24mm f/1.8 — ISO 3200 — 20s for Milky Way. No tracker needed for basic shots. Wide angle for the Karoo's scale (nothing competes with a 14mm here). Arrive at the Three Sisters by golden hour — the rock faces go deep amber. Matjiesfontein at night: 50mm, f/1.4, ambient light from the hotel only.

Day 02Tue 28 Jan~250 km · 2.5 hrs
Matjiesfontein → Cape Town

A short but spectacular drive through the Hex River Valley mountain pass — possibly the most beautiful 30 minutes of driving in the country. The vine-covered valley floor gives way to precipitous peaks, then descends into the Boland. Into the Mother City by early afternoon. Settle in, walk to Camps Bay, let the Atlantic hit you for the first time.

What to do
☀ Scenic Drive
Hex River Pass
Stop at every viewpoint — vine rows, mountain peaks, rivers
🍷 Food + Drink
V&A Waterfront Arrival
Drop bags, grab lunch at the Waterfront with harbour views
🌊 Ocean
Camps Bay First Sunset
Twelve Apostles turn gold. Chinchilla Bar for drinks on the beach
🚵 Walk
Sea Point Promenade
Evening stroll along the Atlantic tidal pools — alive with energy
■ Photographer's Note

The Hex River Valley from above is a 35mm wide shot — vine rows leading to mountain faces. Camps Bay at sunset: shoot from the rocks south of the beach to get the full Twelve Apostles range as backdrop. Bring a 70-200mm to compress the mountains behind the beach bars.

Cape Town

Table Mountain — Cape Town
Day 03Wed 29 Jan
Table Mountain & The City

Start at the cable car at opening (book online the night before — it sells out fast in January). At the top, walk the entire plateau rim for the full 360° — Cape Town, Table Bay, both oceans, and the Twelve Apostles simultaneously. Afternoon in Bo-Kaap for colour, then Bree Street for dinner as the city relaxes into the evening.

What to do
⛰ Landmark
Table Mountain Cable Car
Book online the night before. Go at opening to beat the crowds
🏃 Hike
Skeleton Gorge Descent
Hike up via Platteklip, walk the plateau, descend via Skeleton Gorge
☸ Culture
Bo-Kaap Walking Tour
Cape Malay architecture, mosques, painted houses — perfect for portraits
☸ History
District Six Museum
Powerful testament to apartheid-era forced removals. Essential context
🍴 Food
Bree Street Dinner
Chefs Warehouse + Canteen for tapas, or Shift Espresso for coffee
■ Photographer's Note

Blue hour before sunrise from Signal Hill, facing Table Mountain — this is the iconic CT shot and you need to be there 40 minutes before sunrise. On the plateau: shoot towards the city with a wide angle, or use a 70-200mm to pick out buildings below. Bo-Kaap: ask before photographing people — most locals are happy to pose.

Day 04Thu 30 Jan~150 km loop
Cape Peninsula Day Trip

The greatest coastal drive in Africa. No debate. Chapman's Peak is 9km of cliff-hugging road with the Atlantic 200m below. Cape Point is where the peninsula narrows to a dramatic final cliff and two oceans create wild, chaotic water. Boulders Beach has African penguins literally wandering around your feet on white sand. Bring everything.

What to do
☀ Scenic Drive
Chapman's Peak Drive
Stop at every named viewpoint. Suicide Gorge pullout is the best shot
🌎 Nature
Cape of Good Hope
The actual most south-western point — not Cape Point (common mix-up)
🏙 Landmark
Cape Point Funicular
Ride to the old lighthouse. Views of two oceans meeting in chaos below
🐧 Wildlife
Boulders Beach Penguins
African penguins at arm's length on white sand. Go before 10am
🍴 Food
Kalky's Kalk Bay
Fresh fish and chips on the harbour wall. The real Cape Town meal
🏛 Town
Simon's Town
Victorian naval town with colourful buildings — charming afternoon stop
■ Photographer's Note

Chapman's Peak: the north-facing cliffs are lit from 4pm onward — arrive at the southern end and drive north into the light. Boulders Beach: 70-200mm for penguin portraits. Go early before the boardwalk crowds. Cape Point lighthouse: 16-35mm for the convergence shot looking south and east — shoot from the rocks below the funicular, not just the top.

Day 05Fri 31 Jan
Stellenbosch & Franschhoek Winelands

Into the Cape Winelands for a slow, indulgent day of exceptional wine and food. Stellenbosch in the morning — pick up a wine route map from the tourism office on the main road — then wind through to Franschhoek, SA's culinary capital, for the afternoon. The mountains behind both towns provide constantly shifting light all day.

What to do

■ Photographer's Note

Late afternoon gold in the vineyards is the shot — vine rows with the Franschhoek mountains as backdrop, backlit by 5pm light. 50mm or 85mm, f/2.8 for row compression. Delaire Graff has a sculpture garden that works brilliantly with a wide angle. The Cape Dutch manor house at Boschendal is one of SA's most photogenic buildings — 15 min detour north of Franschhoek.

Day 06Sat 1 Feb
Cape Town Free Day

No schedule. The best Cape Town day is the one where you follow whatever the morning offers. Clifton 4th is quieter than Camps Bay and more beautiful. Kalk Bay in the afternoon for the antique shops, art galleries, and fresh fish. Signal Hill for the sunset that makes every visitor wish they'd stayed longer.

What to do
🌴 Beach

Boulder-sheltered, quieter than Camps Bay. Cold but beautiful. No cars
🎁 Markets

🌄 Shopping

🌞 Sunset

🚵 Walk

The shot most CT photographers chase: blue hour from Mouille Point Beach facing the lighthouse with Table Mountain and Lion's Head in the background. Arrive 20 minutes before sunset, stay 30 minutes after. 24-70mm, tripod. The tidal pools create a perfect foreground reflection. This composition has won multiple international awards.

Garden Route

The Heads — Knysna Lagoon
Day 07Sun 2 Feb~450 km · 5 hrs
Cape Town → Hermanus → Knysna

Head east along the R43 Whale Coast — avoid the N2 and take the longer, coastal scenic route via Kleinmond. Hermanus is SA's whale watching capital; the cliff path above the old harbour is one of the great coastal walks, and southern right whales breach directly below you in January. Arrive Knysna in time for The Heads at golden hour.

What to do
🐳 Wildlife
Hermanus Cliff Path Walk
12km coastal path. Whales breach in Walker Bay right below the cliffs
🏄 Boat
Southern Right Whale Watch
Dyer Island Cruises. January = peak season. 2hr boat trip from Gansbaai nearby
🍴 Food
Harbour House Hermanus
Seafood restaurant built into the cliff face. Views of the bay
🌩 Scenic
Swellendam Town Stop
Oldest town in SA after Cape Town. 30-min coffee stop, Dutch Reformed church
🌞 Golden Hour
Knysna Heads Sunset
Two sandstone cliffs guarding the lagoon. One of the great SA photo spots
■ Photographer's Note

Hermanus: 300-400mm telephoto for whale spray and breach shots from the cliff path. Position yourself at the old harbour lookout — whales tend to surface in the kelp beds 100-200m offshore. Knysna Heads at golden hour: shoot from the Eastern Head viewpoint looking west — the lagoon entrance lights up orange and the forest behind turns dark green against warm sky.

Day 08Mon 3 Feb
Plettenberg Bay

The most beautiful day on the Garden Route. Plettenberg Bay is where the Indian Ocean turns warm and impossibly blue, dolphins come into the bay in pods of 50+, and the Robberg Peninsula extends into the ocean with one of SA's best short hikes — ending at a seal colony that sounds like it escaped from a cartoon.

What to do
🌟 Sunrise
Lookout Beach Dawn
Best sunrise on the Garden Route. The bay is utterly still at 5:45am
🏃 Hike
Robberg Peninsula Loop
4km spectacular loop — seal colony at the point, cliff ledge sections
🐋 Wildlife
Dolphin Watching
Ocean Blue Adventures. Bottlenose dolphins guaranteed in January bays
🚴 Adventure
Keurbooms Lagoon Kayak
Paddle up the river through indigenous forest. Magical and peaceful
🍴 Food
The Lookout Restaurant
Glass-fronted cliff restaurant above Lookout Beach. Lunch with a view
■ Photographer's Note

Robberg Peninsula: the seal colony at the point is best shot with a 200-400mm — they're loud, chaotic, and wonderful. The peninsula silhouette at golden hour from the mainland beach is a wide-angle scene. Lookout Beach: shoot the bay from the Lookout deck with a 24-70mm to capture the full sweep of the bay. Dolphins from the boat: 70-200mm, burst mode, pre-focus on the water surface.

Day 09Tue 4 Feb
Bloukrans Bungee + Tsitsikamma National Park

The world's highest commercial bungee jump at 216m, followed by the raw, ancient beauty of Tsitsikamma. The contrast is deliberate: the adrenaline of free-fall over a river gorge, then the stillness of a 700-year-old yellowwood forest where the only sound is the Indian Ocean against volcanic rock. Both are unforgettable.

What to do
💨 Adrenaline
Bloukrans Bungee Jump
216m — world record. Face Adrenalin. Book online. You have to do it
💪 Adventure
Bridge Arch Walk
No jump option — walk the arch of the bridge over the gorge. Still wild
🌿 Nature
Big Tree Hike
45-min return to a 700-year-old Outeniqua yellowwood. Humbling scale
🌿 Hike
Storms River Mouth Trail
Suspension bridges over the gorge. 1hr return, dramatic coastal scenery
🋔 Swim
Tsitsikamma Tidal Pools
Warm at low tide. Octopus, sea urchins, small fish in natural rock pools
■ Photographer's Note

Bloukrans: Face Adrenalin can photograph your jump for you — their angle from the bridge is better than anything you'll manage yourself. Tsitsikamma suspension bridge: shoot from below at low tide, looking up through the bridge structure with the gorge walls framing. Forest photography: overcast days are best — diffuse light in the yellowwood canopy, long exposures on the rivers. Storms River mouth: blue hour with a 10-stop ND is spectacular.

Day 10Wed 5 Feb~600 km · 6–7 hrs
Eastern Cape → Durban

Long drive day north on the N2. The Eastern Cape unfolds in sweeping, dramatic valleys and occasional glimpses of the Wild Coast. Stop in Jeffrey's Bay — the surf capital of the world — then push north through the former homelands to Durban's warm arrival: the smell of frangipani, curry on the air, and the Indian Ocean sitting at 28°C.

What to do
🏄 Surf

☸ Museum

☕ Coffee

Durban Golden Mile

■ Photographer's Note

Supertubes surf action requires 300-400mm at minimum for compression from the beach. Pre-focus on the peak and wait — the waves are mechanical in their consistency. JBay main beach at blue hour: wide angle with the wave-worn rocks as foreground. Durban arrival: shoot the Golden Mile beachfront from the pier at dusk — the curve of the coast with city lights is a classic long-exposure shot.

Golden Mile — Durban Beachfront
Day 11Thu 6 Feb
Durban — uShaka & Indian Quarter

Durban is a city that assaults every sense in the best possible way. uShaka Marine World houses Africa's most impressive aquarium in a giant ruined ship. The Indian Quarter on Grey Street is where the real city reveals itself — spice markets, roti shops, fabric sellers, and curry houses that exist nowhere else in Africa.

What to do
🐠 Marine
uShaka Aquarium
World-class aquarium in a fake shipwreck. The shark tank walkthrough is wild
🌅 Market
Victoria Street Market
Spices, fabrics, curios — Durban's iconic covered market. Negotiate everything
🍔 Food
Bunny Chow at Goundens
Grey Street, original bunny chow — lamb or bean curry in a hollowed bread loaf
🌍 Gardens
Durban Botanic Gardens
Oldest surviving botanic garden in Africa. Cycad collection is exceptional
🍻 Dinner
Billy the Bum's Florida Rd
Classic Durban institution on the best restaurant strip in the city
■ Photographer's Note

Victoria Street Market: ask before shooting portraits — most traders are enthusiastic if you do. The spice stall colour contrasts are extraordinary with a 50mm f/1.8. uShaka aquarium: IBIS 5+ required, no flash. The sharks-through-glass shot works best from the tunnel, not the viewing gallery. Blue hour at the Golden Mile: wide angle from the pier, 30s exposure to smooth the surf.

Day 12Fri 7 Feb~2 hrs north of Durban
iSimangaliso Wetland Park

The most biodiverse place in Africa. UNESCO World Heritage Site and the only ecosystem on earth where hippos, leatherback turtles, flamingos, rhinos, and whale sharks share the same environment. The sunrise estuary safari is one of the great wildlife experiences on the continent — hippos at 3-5 metres from the boat, guaranteed.

What to do
🐸 Wildlife

6am St Lucia Estuary. Hippos at 3m — guaranteed. Book via iSimangaliso operator
🚵 Self-Drive

Self-drive at dawn. Flamingos on the pans, nyala in the reeds, fish eagles
🋔 Snorkel
Cape Vidal Reef Snorkelling
Coral reef snorkelling at low tide — only accessible inside the park
🐢 Wildlife
Turtle Nesting Beach
January is peak leatherback nesting. Guided night walks available
🐟 Birdwatch
False Bay Flamingos
Greater and lesser flamingo flocks on the freshwater lake. Hundreds strong
■ Photographer's Note

Hippo boat safari: 70-200mm f/2.8. The light at 6am is low and golden — use ISO 1600-3200 to maintain shutter speed for moving subjects. Cape Vidal: underwater housing or a waterproof compact for snorkelling shots. Eastern Shores at dawn: wide angle for the pan landscapes with flamingo flocks. Turtle tracks at dawn: wide angle, get low to the sand for scale — the tracks are 2m wide.

Day 13Sat 8 Feb~2.5 hrs from Durban
Hluhluwe-iMfolozi Game Reserve

Africa's oldest game reserve, established in 1895, and the place that saved the white rhino from extinction. A self-drive produces consistently excellent Big Five sightings in a more intimate setting than Kruger — with fewer than 5% of Kruger's visitor numbers but comparable game density. The landscape shifts from open savanna to dense thicket to riverine forest within minutes.

What to do
🕑 Dawn Drive
Hluhluwe Section at Dawn
Northern section. White rhino, giraffe, nyala, and elephant within the first hour
🐾 Wildlife
iMfolozi Wild Dog Territory
Southern section. African wild dog, lion, and buffalo country
🌟 Scenic
Hilltop Camp Viewing Deck
Elevated deck over the valley. Waterbuck, warthog, and constant bird activity
🐾 Guided
Guided Wilderness Walk
Book at reception on arrival. On foot with armed ranger — extraordinary experience
🌞 Evening
Dusk Game Drive
Return late afternoon for the second game drive. Predator activity peaks at dusk
■ Photographer's Note

White rhino mother and calf is the signature shot — go wide to include the thicket landscape behind. The golden grass at dusk with backlighting is extraordinary. 500mm+ for comfortable rhino approach distances. Guided walk photography: lighter kit — 70-200mm maximum, no tripod. The experience of tracking big game on foot produces authentic documentary images that tower-vehicle shots can't match.

The Oyster Box Hotel is a Durban institution: arrive at 5pm for sundowners on the ocean-facing terrace.

Drakensberg & Home

The Amphitheatre — Royal Natal NP
Day 15Mon 10 Feb~3.5 hrs from Durban
Durban → Royal Natal National Park

The drive inland from Durban is one of the great South African landscape transitions — subtropical coast to the foothills of the Drakensberg in under four hours. Royal Natal NP sits at the base of the Amphitheatre: a 5km basalt cliff wall rising 1,100m from the valley floor in a single dramatic step. Nothing else in Africa looks like this.

What to do
🌞 Golden Hour
Amphitheatre Sundowner
The basalt wall goes copper-red in the last 30 mins of light. Position yourself at the Tendele viewpoint
🏃 Hike
Cascades Trail
Easy 1hr walk through indigenous forest to a series of small waterfalls
🏃 Hike
Tugela Falls Base Hike
5km return, 2hrs. Trail to the base of the 947m falls — world's second tallest
🏠 Stay
Tendele Hutted Camp
Mid-range chalets inside the park with direct Amphitheatre views. Book ahead
■ Photographer's Note

The Amphitheatre at golden hour is one of the greatest photography opportunities in Africa. The basalt wall shifts from grey to copper to deep red in the final 30 minutes before sunset. Set up at the Tendele chalets viewpoint — tripod, 24mm, f/8, bracket 3 exposures. Blue hour is equally powerful: the cliff holds the last light long after the valley is dark. In January, storm clouds build over the plateau by 3pm and create extraordinary dramatic skies.

Day 16Tue 11 Feb
Full Day in the Drakensberg

A full day at altitude. Wake before dawn and watch the Amphitheatre face emerge from the dark. The Gorge Trail takes you deep into the basalt walls through natural swimming pools and ancient San rock art. The scale is staggering — you feel genuinely small against the cliff, which is the point. Evening stargazing at this elevation is exceptional.

What to do
🌟 Pre-Dawn
Amphitheatre Sunrise Watch
Position at Tendele viewpoint before dawn. The face emerges from the dark slowly
🏃 Hike
Gorge Trail (Moderate)
4.5km return. Swimming pools inside the gorge. San rock art en route
🏄 Activity
Fly Fishing — Tugela River
Trout fishing in the Tugela. Tendele can arrange permits and guide
🚵 Drive
Cathedral Peak Day Drive
1hr to a different berg section. Dramatic isolated peak, good afternoon light
🌌 Night
Milky Way Session
High altitude + zero light pollution = some of the best Milky Way photography in SA
■ Photographer's Note

The Gorge swimming pool mirror shot: bring a 16-35mm and shoot from the water level looking back up the gorge — the cliff walls frame the sky perfectly. For star trails at elevation: 24mm f/1.8, ISO 1600, 30s intervals over 2 hours for a complete arc. The reduced atmospheric interference at 1,400m ASL gives noticeably sharper pinpoint stars than the coast. January storms mean pre-storm golden light with dramatic cumulonimbus behind the Amphitheatre — wait for this combination.

Day 17Wed 12 Feb~5 hrs total
Midlands Meander → Johannesburg

The perfect close to the loop. The Midlands Meander through the KZN foothills is everything the trip was not — slow, green, gentle. Rolling hills, craft studios, fly-fishing rivers, and cheese farms. A long farm lunch somewhere in Nottingham Road, a stop at Ardmore Ceramics (the most celebrated craft studio in southern Africa), and then the N3 north. Home.

What to do
🏘 Craft
Ardmore Ceramics Studio
World-famous studio near Champagne Castle. Extraordinary beadwork and ceramics
🍲 Food

Nottingham Road. Farm breakfast on the veranda overlooking the trout dam
🧀 Food

🏬 Waterfall
Howick Falls
70m waterfall 5 minutes from the N3. Quick stop, big impact
🏘 History

SA's second city. Victorian architecture and the Mandela capture site nearby
■ Photographer's Note

Howick Falls: shoot from the top AND the bottom. The base view (short hike down) gives a dramatic upward perspective that the tourist viewpoint above doesn't. Long exposure (10-stop ND, f/16, 30s) in the morning light smooths the falls beautifully. Morning mist on the Midlands hills: depart Tendele before 6am for the first hour of mist in the valleys — one of the most atmospheric landscapes in KZN.
