Phase 01 — January 27–28

Departure & Karoo

2 Days · ~1,000 km
The Great Karoo — Northern Cape
Day 01Mon 27 Jan
Johannesburg → Matjiesfontein

Depart before 5am to beat JHB traffic and watch the Highveld give way to the vast, alien silence of the Karoo. Overnight in Matjiesfontein — a perfectly preserved Victorian ghost town that time forgot. Zero light pollution. The Southern Milky Way core rises after midnight and stays vivid until 4am.

Things to Do
🌞
Pre-Dawn Departure
Leave JHB by 5am — skip the traffic, hit sunrise on open road
🏔
Three Sisters Stop
3 solitary koppies rising from the plains — iconic Karoo photo stop
🏠
Lord Milner Hotel
Victorian colonial hotel, cold beer, great food — only place in town
📷
Matjiesfontein Village Walk
Old steam train, village museum, colonial architecture, ghost-town vibes
⭐
Milky Way Stargazing
Set alarm for 2am. Bortle Class 2 skies — among the darkest in Africa
📷
Long Exposure Night Shoot
Old train + Milky Way arch. 16mm, f/2, ISO 3200, 20 sec. Magic.
Photographer’s Note

Bring your widest lens and sturdiest tripod. The galactic core rises from the south-east around midnight and peaks at 2am. The old steam locomotive at the station makes a striking foreground. Shoot between 2–4am — the core starts fading after 4:30am.

~750 km · 7 hrs StargazingVictorian HeritagePhotography
Day 02Tue 28 Jan
Matjiesfontein → Cape Town

A 2.5-hour drive through the spectacular Hex River Valley — vineyards flanked by the Hex River Mountains, one of the most beautiful valleys in South Africa. Into Cape Town by early afternoon. Arrive, drop bags, and walk straight to Camps Bay. The Atlantic at golden hour. Your first night in the Mother City.

Things to Do
⛰
Hex River Valley Pass
Multiple lay-bys with vineyard and mountain views — stop at every one
🚹
Paarl Winelands Stop
Quick detour to Paarl — KWV Brandy Cellar or Grande Provence
🌊
V&A Waterfront
Two Oceans Aquarium, harbour seals, craft beer at The Beerhouse
🏌
Sea Point Promenade
3km oceanfront walk with sea pools — great intro to CT coastal life
🏠
Camps Bay Sunset
Twelve Apostles mountain range goes amber behind you at 7:30pm
🍕
Ocean Basket / Bombay
Long Street dinner — Ocean Basket for seafood, Bombay for curry
Photographer’s Note

The Hex River Valley vineyards are at peak green in January — lush, full growth against dramatic mountain backdrops. Stop at the Huguenot Tunnel viewpoint before descending. Camps Bay at golden hour: shoot from the southern end of the beach facing north-west — the mountains frame the sunset perfectly.

Phase 02 — January 29 – February 1

Cape Town

4 Days · 6 Nights
Table Mountain — Cape Town
Day 03Wed 29 Jan
Table Mountain & Cape Town City

Table Mountain at opening time — cable car or the Platteklip Gorge hike. 360° views of the city, both oceans, and the full Cape Peninsula. Afternoon in Bo-Kaap for the colourful street photography, then Zeitz MOCAA for contemporary African art. Long Street and Bree Street for the city’s best food and nightlife.

Things to Do
⛰
Table Mountain Cable Car
Book online the day before — Jan queues are 2+ hrs without a booking
🏃
Platteklip Gorge Hike
2hr hike up, 45min cable car down — best of both worlds approach
🏠
Bo-Kaap
Cape Malay Quarter — colourful houses, spice shops, koeksisters
🌐
Zeitz MOCAA
Africa’s premier contemporary art museum — stunning converted grain silo
🏛
District Six Museum
Powerful apartheid-era history. 2 hrs. Essential Cape Town context.
🍽
Bree Street Food Scene
The Test Kitchen, Pot Luck Club, Shortmarket Club — book ahead
Photographer’s Note

Be at the cable car station 15 minutes before opening (8am in Jan). The summit at 8am is nearly crowd-free. Fog rolls in from the south by midday in January and can sock the summit in within minutes — go early or late afternoon (4pm light is extraordinary). The tablecloth effect is itself a shot, but plan for clear mornings first.

Day 04Thu 30 Jan
Cape Peninsula Full Day

The greatest coastal drive in Africa. Chapman’s Peak — seven kilometres of road blasted into sheer cliffs dropping straight into the Atlantic. Cape Point at the tip of the peninsula. Boulders Beach for African penguins on white sand. The whole loop is ~150km but delivers constantly.

Things to Do
🏁
Chapman’s Peak Drive
Start at 7:30am sharp — beat the tour buses, toll opens at 7am
🚤
Hout Bay Seal Island
30-min boat trip, 75,000 Cape fur seals on a sea stack — R150
📍
Cape of Good Hope Sign
The classic shot. Go early — by 10am tour buses crowd the sign
🔍
Cape Point Lighthouse
Funicular or steep hike. The old lighthouse viewpoint is exceptional
🍎
Boulders Beach Penguins
African penguins at close range. 2,500 birds. Go before 9am.
🍽
Kalk Bay Harbour
Kalky’s fish & chips on the quay. Best in Cape Town, full stop.
Photographer’s Note

Chapman’s Peak faces west — morning light comes from behind you shooting southward. The cliffs are best shot from the north-facing lay-bys. Cape Point gets extraordinary light between 9–11am. Bring a 70–200mm for the penguins at Boulders — you can get very close but the compression makes the colony look massive.

Day 05Fri 31 Jan
Stellenbosch & Franschhoek

Into the Cape Winelands — one of the great food and wine regions of the world. Oak-lined streets, Cape Dutch manor houses, and estates that hold their own against anything in Bordeaux or Burgundy. Stellenbosch wine route in the morning, Franschhoek valley in the afternoon. January is harvest season: the estates are alive.

Things to Do
🍷
Tokara Estate
Top of Helshoogte Pass — incredible valley views, exceptional Chardonnay
👑
Delaire Graff Estate
Most beautiful estate in SA. Art collection + fine dining. Book ahead.
🍺
Jordan Wine Estate
Dam-side restaurant with duck terrine. The Cobbler’s best kept secret.
🚊
Franschhoek Wine Tram
Hop-on hop-off tram through the valley. Bleu Route is the best. Book online.
🎲
Haute Cabrière
Dramatic mountain cave cellar. MCC pairing — bottomless bubbles + cheese
🏛
La Motte Art Museum
Pierneef artworks in a Cape Dutch homestead — free with tasting
Photographer’s Note

Tokara sits at the top of the Helshoogte Pass — the valley views from the terrace at golden hour are among the finest in the Western Cape. The vineyards below are at peak green in January (harvest begins late Feb). Franschhoek main street at dusk, with the Huguenot Memorial lit up, is a quiet, beautiful shot most people miss.

Wine TastingCape Dutch ArchitectureFine DiningWine Tram
Day 06Sat 1 Feb
Cape Town Free Day

Slow morning, then pick your adventure. Lion’s Head hike for the 360° summit view of the peninsula, or the Sea Point rock pools. Camps Bay beach in the afternoon. Signal Hill for golden hour. Cape Town’s nightlife on Bree Street or De Waterkant is genuinely world-class — don’t miss it.

Things to Do
🏃
Lion’s Head Hike
2.5hr circular. Start 4pm for golden hour at the 669m summit
🌊
Sea Point Rock Pools
Free tidal pools along the promenade. Cool Atlantic morning swim.
🏖
Clifton 4th Beach
The most beautiful city beach in the world. Arrive by 11am for a spot.
🎼
Robben Island Ferry
Nelson Mandela’s cell, former political prison — deeply moving 4hr tour
🌚
Signal Hill Sunset
Park on Signal Hill Road at 6pm and watch the city go gold below you
🍻
De Waterkant / Bree St
Orphanage Cocktail Emporium or Publik Wine Bar for world-class drinks
Photographer’s Note

Lion’s Head at sunset in January: the city below glows amber, the Atlantic goes rose-pink, and Table Mountain frames everything to the south-east. The 2.5hr circular hike has chains and ladders near the summit — exhilarating. Check the lunar calendar; the full moon hike is a Cape Town bucket-list experience.

Phase 03 — February 2–5

Garden Route

4 Days · ~1,600 km
The Heads — Knysna Lagoon
Day 07Sun 2 Feb
Cape Town → Hermanus → Knysna

Head east along the Whale Coast. The cliff path above Walker Bay in Hermanus is extraordinary — southern right whales breach below you in January. The Hemel-en-Aarde valley just outside town has excellent wine stops. Continue east on the N2 through Swellendam and the Overberg. Arrive Knysna for golden hour at The Heads.

Things to Do
🎺
Betty’s Bay Penguins
Less visited than Boulders, equally special. 30min before Hermanus.
🐳
Hermanus Cliff Path Walk
12km coastal path. Whales breach below in Jan. High tide = closest action.
🐸
Walker Bay Whale Watching
Southern right whales year-round in Jan. Cliff views are free — boats too.
🍷
Hemel-en-Aarde Valley Wine
Hamilton Russell Vineyards — best Pinot Noir in Africa. 15min from Hermanus.
🏛
Stanford Village
Charming river village, 30min from Hermanus. Coffee at Mariana’s.
🌈
Knysna Heads Sunset
Drive up to the viewpoint above the eastern head. Golden hour is unmissable.
Photographer’s Note

The Hermanus cliff path faces north, so morning light comes from behind you shooting east. Check the tide tables — whales breach closest to the cliffs at high tide. Use a 400mm or longer to compress the whale spout against the cliff backdrop. The Knysna Heads at golden hour from the eastern headland road is one of the best single-shot locations on the Garden Route.

Day 08Mon 3 Feb
Plettenberg Bay

The jewel of the Garden Route. Blue Flag beaches, warm Indian Ocean, and common dolphins that come right into the bay in January. The Robberg Nature Reserve peninsula hike is one of the best walks in SA — seal colony, dramatic cliffs, two oceans visible from the narrow neck. Don’t skip it.

Things to Do
🏃
Robberg Peninsula Hike
3–4hr loop, seal colony, narrow ocean neck. Check tide tables. R60 entry.
🏈
Lookout Beach
Best Blue Flag beach on the Garden Route. Dolphins swim past at 7am.
🚤
Dolphin & Whale Boat Cruise
2hr ocean cruise. Jan = dolphins guaranteed, whales frequent.
🐰
Monkeyland
Free-roaming primates in forest sanctuary. 1hr guided walk — incredible.
🐦
Birds of Eden Aviary
World’s largest free-flight bird aviary under one dome. Next door to Monkeyland.
🌄
Keurbooms Lagoon
Kayak 8km up the lagoon through forest — hire from Keurbooms River Ferry
Photographer’s Note

Robberg Peninsula is one of the great photography walks in South Africa. The narrow neck between two stretches of ocean — Indian Ocean both sides, cliffs above and below — is extraordinary at low tide when you can walk right out to the point. Bring a telephoto for the seal colony (hundreds of Cape fur seals, stacked) and a wide for the headland-to-headland shot.

Robberg HikeDolphin CruiseBlue Flag BeachWildlife Sanctuary
Day 09Tue 4 Feb
Bloukrans Bungee + Tsitsikamma

The most adrenaline-charged day of the trip. The world’s highest commercial bungee jump at 216m off the Bloukrans River Bridge — even if you don’t jump, the bridge arch walk is exhilarating. Then into Tsitsikamma: ancient yellowwood forest, a rocky Indian Ocean coastline, and the Storms River gorge suspension bridge.

Things to Do
❗
Bloukrans Bungee — 216m
World’s highest. ~R1,950. Book 48hrs ahead at faceadrenalin.co.za
🗺
Bloukrans Bridge Walk
Non-jumper arch walk for the same views. ~R450. No strapping required.
🌳
Tsitsikamma Yellowwood Forest
Ancient forest walk. Trees over 800 years old. Ethereal light through the canopy.
🌃
Storms River Gorge Bridge
800m suspension bridge walk into the gorge. Opens 7am. Most dramatic at dawn.
🏂
Big Swing Tsitsikamma
High-wire swing above the canopy — less extreme than bungee, equally thrilling
🚣
Storms River Mouth Kayak
Kayak the last 2km of the river into the gorge mouth — dramatic walls, turquoise water
Photographer’s Note

The Storms River gorge suspension bridge at dawn — mist rising from the gorge, yellowwood canopy on both sides, turquoise river below. The park opens at 7am; be at the gate at opening. The light through the ancient forest is extraordinary in the early morning — dappled, soft, dimensional. Bring a tripod for the slow-shutter gorge shots.

Day 10Wed 5 Feb
Eastern Cape → Durban

Long drive day north on the N2 through the Eastern Cape. The optional stop at Jeffrey’s Bay is absolutely worth 2 hours — world-famous surf break, excellent coffee, and a particular laid-back energy that belongs nowhere else in SA. Arrive Durban to warm tropical air, the smell of curry drifting from every street, and the Indian Ocean at 28°C.

Things to Do
🏄
Supertubes — Jeffrey’s Bay
Watch world-class surfers on one of the best right-hand point breaks on earth
🍷
J-Bay Coffee Scene
Walskipper or Stomping Grounds — genuinely excellent coffee in a surf town
🏄
J-Bay Surf Museum
History of surfing in SA — unexpectedly brilliant. Free entry.
🏖
Main Beach Walk
Palm-lined beachfront, warm Indian Ocean. Quick swim before the long drive.
🌊
Arrive Durban Golden Mile
Indian Ocean at 28°C. Drop bags, get straight in the water.
🍕
First Bunny Chow
Durban’s iconic Indian curry in a hollowed loaf. Govender’s, Grey Street.
Photographer’s Note

Supertubes at Jeffrey’s Bay in January is peak surfing season. The right-hand point break peeling for 300+ metres is one of the world’s most photographed waves. Bring your longest lens — 400mm minimum for shooting from the beach. The wave faces east, so morning light is ideal. Position yourself at the end of Point Road.

Phase 04 — February 6–9

Durban & KwaZulu-Natal

4 Days · Big Five + Ocean
Golden Mile — Durban, KwaZulu-Natal
Day 11Thu 6 Feb
uShaka Marine World & Indian Quarter

Africa’s largest marine theme park in the morning — a genuinely world-class aquarium built into the hull of a fake shipwreck. Afternoon deep into Durban’s extraordinary Indian heritage: the Victoria Street Market, bunny chow on Grey Street, and the Moses Mabhida Stadium arch climb at golden hour for the best view in Durban.

Things to Do
🎰
uShaka Aquarium
Shark exhibit, shipwreck tunnels, touch pools. 3hrs minimum inside.
🐵
Dolphin Shows
2 shows daily. The pool is enormous — front row gets wet.
🏛
Moses Mabhida Arch Climb
187 steps to the arch apex. 360° views. Golden hour = exceptional. R145.
🍅
Victoria Street Market
300 stalls: spices, fabrics, Zulu beadwork, leather. Bargain hard.
🍜
Bunny Chow
Govender’s, Grey Street. Bean bunny for vegetarians. Lamb for the brave.
🍻
Wilson’s Wharf Sundowner
Harbour-side cocktails as the cargo ships come in. Excellent seafood too.
Photographer’s Note

Moses Mabhida arch climb at golden hour gives you the single best panoramic shot in Durban — the entire beachfront, Golden Mile, and Indian Ocean lit warm below. Book the climb from 5:30pm in January for the light. The Victoria Street Market interior is richly photogenic — ask vendors before shooting, most are happy to pose.

uShaka Marine WorldStadium Arch ClimbVictoria St MarketIndian Quarter
Day 12Fri 7 Feb
iSimangaliso Wetland Park

Day trip north to St Lucia. UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Africa’s most biodiverse ecosystems. The only place on earth where rhinos share habitat with whale sharks. January is peak turtle nesting season — a night turtle tour watching a leatherback turtle lay eggs is a once-in-a-lifetime experience. Book weeks ahead.

Things to Do
🏞
Sunrise Hippo Boat Safari
5:30am estuary cruise. Hippos + crocs + birds + mist. Book at reception night before.
🎌
Night Turtle Tour
Jan–Feb peak nesting. Leatherback and loggerhead turtles. Book weeks ahead.
🌊
Cape Vidal Snorkelling
Coral reef inside the park. Warm water, sea turtles, tropical fish. 28°C.
🦔
Whale Shark Snorkel Tour
Jan can deliver whale sharks off Sodwana Bay. 2hr drive north of St Lucia.
🏃
iSimangaliso Walking Trails
Guided walks with armed ranger through hippo and croc territory. Extraordinary.
🐸
St Lucia Town Walk
Hippos wander the streets of St Lucia town at night. Not a joke.
Photographer’s Note

The sunrise estuary boat safari is among the most photogenic wildlife experiences in South Africa — thick mist on the water, hippos lifting their heads, pink light filtering through the mangroves. Bring a 70–200mm minimum. The night turtle tour is a totally different kind of shot: one headlamp, a leatherback turtle, and absolute silence. You’ll never forget it.

Day 13Sat 8 Feb
Hluhluwe-iMfolozi Game Reserve

Africa’s oldest proclaimed game reserve, and the place that single-handedly saved the white rhino from extinction. Self-drive on well-maintained tar roads, entering at gate-opening (5:30am). The Hluhluwe section (north) is lush and good for elephant, leopard and buffalo. The iMfolozi section (south) is more open and better for rhino and lion.

Things to Do
🕑
Dawn Gate Entry — 5:30am
Be at the gate at opening. First hour of light = best game movement.
🦍
White Rhino Tracking
iMfolozi section. The Memorial Gate loop is the best rhino road in SA.
🦁
Lion Sightings
Ask rangers at the gate where lions were spotted that morning. Share info.
🐘
Elephant Herds
Hluhluwe section north — family herds at waterholes by 9am.
🐾
Leopard Watch
Hluhluwe section, wooded ridges. Dawn and dusk. Patience rewarded.
🏜
Hilltop Camp Viewpoint
Have lunch at Hilltop Camp — the valley views over the reserve are exceptional
Photographer’s Note

The golden hour light in Hluhluwe is extraordinary — acacia trees backlit, rhino silhouettes on the ridgeline, warm amber through the grass. A 400mm is the minimum for usable game shots from a vehicle. Photograph through the open window on a beanbag for stability. Early January mornings are crisp and the low light is exceptional for wildlife.

Day 14Sun 9 Feb
Umhlanga & Durban Nightlife

Last Durban day — slow it down. Umhlanga Rocks has the iconic red lighthouse, the Whalebone Pier, and some of the best seafood restaurants on the KZN coast. Sunset from the pier is genuinely beautiful. Florida Road for dinner. Durban’s nightlife scene punches well above its weight — a proper send-off before the mountains tomorrow.

Things to Do
🌙
Umhlanga Sunrise Walk
Golden hour at the promenade before the crowds. Lighthouse facing east = perfect.
⛩
Whalebone Pier
Walk to the end at high tide. The pier faces east — sunrise here is spectacular.
🏖
Ballito Beach
30min north of Umhlanga. Quieter, rockier, beautiful tidal pools at low tide.
🍽
Ile Maurice Restaurant
Best seafood in Umhlanga. Mauritian-Indian fusion. Book two days ahead.
🍻
Florida Road Strip
Durban’s best restaurant mile: Havana, 9th Avenue Bistro, Tashas
🎶
Durban Nightlife
Village Walk or Point precinct. Durban club culture has deep roots in house music.
Photographer’s Note

Umhlanga Rocks at sunrise before the beach crowds is quietly magical — the red lighthouse in warm first light, the warm Indian Ocean behind, pelicans on the pier. Set the alarm for 5am. Then return at golden hour (7:30pm) for the sunset light on the lighthouse face and the palm-lined promenade.

Phase 05 — February 10–12

Drakensberg & Home

3 Days · Mountains
Royal Natal National Park — Drakensberg
Day 15Mon 10 Feb
Durban → Royal Natal NP

Drive inland on the N3. The shift from subtropical coast to mountain is one of the most dramatic transitions in southern Africa. Royal Natal National Park sits at the base of the Amphitheatre — a 5km basalt cliff wall rising 1,100m from the valley floor. Stop at Howick Falls on the way; it’s one of the most beautiful waterfalls in the country.

Things to Do
👴
Howick Falls
67m waterfall, 30min off the N3. The gorge is beautiful, faces east — go morning.
🏝
Midlands Preview Stop
Rawdon’s Hotel in Nottingham Road — craft beer + farm lunch preview
🌟
Amphitheatre Viewpoint
Drive to the Royal Natal viewpoint — first sight of the 5km basalt wall.
🏃
Tugela Falls Base Hike
4km, 2 hrs. World’s 2nd highest waterfall. The gorge photography is extraordinary.
🏠
Thendele Camp Check-in
SANParks chalets at the base of the Amphitheatre. Book far in advance.
🌈
Amphitheatre Sundowner
Sit on the chalet stoep and watch the basalt wall go from orange to purple.
Photographer’s Note

Howick Falls faces east — best light in the morning with the sun behind you. The gorge below is dramatic and often photographed from above, but descending to the base (steep path) gives you a completely different shot. Tugela Falls from the base of the gorge: use a wide angle and long shutter on a tripod to capture the cascade against the basalt cliff. The scale is staggering.

Day 16Tue 11 Feb
Full Day in the Drakensberg

Dawn at the Amphitheatre face, then a full day in the mountains. The Gorge Trail takes you 8km deep into the basalt wall — mountain pools, cold clear streams, and a sense of scale that makes everything feel small. January means lush green valleys and dramatic afternoon thunderstorm clouds that build and break over the plateau. The photography is relentless.

Things to Do
🌞
Amphitheatre Sunrise
Pre-dawn hike to the face. The first rays on the 5km cliff are extraordinary.
🏃
Gorge Trail (8km)
Full day hike into the Amphitheatre gorge. Chain ladders near the top. Bring layers.
🚶
Mountain Pools Swimming
Cold, clear, deep. After 10km of mountain trails, the most deserved swim in SA.
🌳
San Rock Paintings
Bushman rock art sites inside Royal Natal. Guided walks available at camp.
⛈
Thunderstorm Photography
Set up in shelter at 3pm. Jan storms are spectacular — lightning on the escarpment.
🏛
Cascades Trail (Easier)
Gentler 6km option if the Gorge is too much. Beautiful waterfall finale.
Photographer’s Note

Bring your widest lens for the Amphitheatre — nothing in your bag captures the full 5km scale of the wall. The afternoon thunderstorms over the Drakensberg in January are some of the most dramatic weather photography in southern Africa. Set up in a sheltered spot by 3pm and wait. When sun breaks through storm clouds onto the basalt face at 5pm, the light is indescribable.

Day 17Wed 12 Feb
Midlands Meander → Johannesburg

The perfect way to close the loop. A gentle drive home through the KZN Midlands — rolling green hills, craft studios, cheese farms, fly-fishing rivers, and a long unhurried farm lunch. The Midlands has a quiet, green beauty that most people drive straight through on the N3. Take your time. Then up the highway and home by evening. Cards full. Trip done.

Things to Do
🍽
Rawdon’s Hotel Lunch
Farm-style restaurant in Nottingham Road. Craft beer brewed on site. Excellent.
🍨
Swissland Cheese Farm
Award-winning artisan cheese made on the farm. 20 varieties. Buy for the road.
🏎
Midlands Pottery Studios
Ardmore Ceramic Art — extraordinary hand-painted Zulu-themed ceramics
🎬
Howick Village Coffee
The Old Courthouse Bistro for a final flat white before the N3 home
🏃
Albert Falls Dam Walk
Quick nature walk with valley views — 30min, right off the R103
🏠
Johannesburg Home
N3 north from Hilton. Home by 7pm. Cards full. Loop complete.
Photographer’s Note

The Midlands morning mist lifts from the valleys around 9am in January. The rolling green hills with morning fog burning off are quietly beautiful — a gentle, unexpected final chapter after the drama of the Drakensberg. The R103 through the Meander is slower than the N3 but the light on the valley farms in the morning is worth the extra 20 minutes.
