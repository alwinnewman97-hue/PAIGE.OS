Departure & Karoo

Jan 27–28 · 2 Days
The Karoo — Northern Cape
Day 01Mon 27 Jan
Johannesburg → Matjiesfontein

Depart before dawn. The N1 south swallows you into the Highveld then flattens dramatically into the Karoo. Overnight in Matjiesfontein — a perfectly preserved Victorian ghost town with zero light pollution. The Milky Way here is extraordinary.

~750 km · 7 hrs Karoo Overnight Stargazing Lord Milner Hotel
Day 02Tue 28 Jan
Matjiesfontein → Cape Town

A 2.5-hour drive through the dramatic Hex River Valley mountain pass. Into the Mother City by early afternoon. Walk straight to Camps Bay for the first Atlantic sunset — the Twelve Apostles mountains go gold behind you.

~250 km · 2.5 hrs Hex River Valley Pass Camps Bay Sunset V&A Waterfront

Cape Town

Jan 29 – Feb 1 · 4 Days
Table Mountain — Cape Town
Day 03Wed 29 Jan
Table Mountain & The City

Cable car at opening, or hike — 360° views of Cape Town, Table Bay, and both oceans. Afternoon in Bo-Kaap for the colourful street photography. Long Street for dinner and drinks as the city comes alive.

Table Mountain Bo-Kaap Street Photography Long Street Nightlife
Day 04Thu 30 Jan
Cape Peninsula Day Trip

The greatest coastal drive in Africa. Chapman's Peak — cliffs dropping straight into the Atlantic. Cape Point at the peninsula's tip. Boulders Beach for African penguins on white sand. Bring every lens — this day delivers constantly.

~150 km loop Chapman's Peak Drive Cape Point NR Boulders Beach Penguins
Day 05Fri 31 Jan
Stellenbosch & Franschhoek

Into the Cape Winelands. Oak-lined streets, Cape Dutch manor houses, and world-class wine. Stellenbosch wine route in the morning, then Haute Cabrière perched dramatically above Franschhoek valley for an afternoon MCC pairing.

Stellenbosch Wine Route Delaire Graff Estate Haute Cabrière Franschhoek Wine Tram
Day 06Sat 1 Feb
Cape Town Free Day

Slow Camps Bay morning. Kalk Bay in the afternoon — fishing harbour, vintage shops, Kalky's fish and chips on the quay. Signal Hill for golden hour over the city. De Waterkant or Bree Street for nightlife.

Camps Bay Beach Kalk Bay Harbour Signal Hill Sunset De Waterkant

Garden Route

Feb 2–5 · 4 Days
The Heads — Knysna
Day 07Sun 2 Feb
Cape Town → Hermanus → Knysna

Head east along the Whale Coast. The Hermanus cliff path above Walker Bay is exceptional — southern right whales breach below you in January. Continue east on the N2. Arrive Knysna for golden hour at The Heads: two massive sandstone cliffs guarding the lagoon.

~450 km · 5 hrs Hermanus Cliff Path Whale Watching Knysna Heads Sunset
Day 08Mon 3 Feb
Plettenberg Bay

The jewel of the Garden Route. Blue Flag beaches, warm Indian Ocean, and dolphins that come right into the bay in January. The Lookout Restaurant sits on the cliff above Lookout Beach with one of the finest coastal views in the country.

Lookout Beach Dolphin Watching The Lookout Restaurant Keurbooms Lagoon
Day 09Tue 4 Feb
Bloukrans Bungee + Tsitsikamma

World's highest commercial bungee at 216m off Bloukrans River Bridge — book online in advance. Then into Tsitsikamma: ancient yellowwood forest, rocky Indian Ocean coastline, and the Storms River gorge suspension bridge. One of the most photogenic places in South Africa.

Bloukrans Bungee — 216m Tsitsikamma NP Storms River Gorge Book ahead online
Day 10Wed 5 Feb
Eastern Cape → Durban

Long drive north on the N2 through the Eastern Cape. Optional stop at Jeffrey's Bay — world-famous surf break, great coffee, vibrant beach town energy. Arrive Durban to warm air, the smell of curry, and the Indian Ocean at 28°C.

~600 km · 6–7 hrs Jeffrey's Bay Stop Supertubes Beach Arrive Durban

Durban & KwaZulu-Natal

Feb 6–9 · 4 Days
Golden Mile — Durban
Day 11Thu 6 Feb
uShaka Marine World & Indian Quarter

Africa's largest marine theme park in the morning — incredible aquarium, shipwreck exhibit, dolphin shows. Afternoon deep into Durban's Indian heritage: Victoria Street Market, bunny chow on Grey Street, spice traders along Prince Edward. A city like nowhere else in SA.

uShaka Marine World Victoria Street Market Bunny Chow Indian Quarter
Day 12Fri 7 Feb
iSimangaliso Wetland Park

Day trip north to St Lucia. UNESCO World Heritage Site — the only place on earth where rhinos share habitat with whale sharks. Book a sunrise estuary boat safari: hippos at close range are near-guaranteed. Cape Vidal beach inside the park for snorkelling.

~2 hrs north of Durban Sunrise Hippo Boat Safari Cape Vidal Beach Snorkelling UNESCO Heritage
Day 13Sat 8 Feb
Hluhluwe-iMfolozi Game Reserve

Africa's oldest game reserve and the place that saved the white rhino from extinction. Self-drive game drives at dawn and dusk. Rhino, elephant, lion, leopard and buffalo all confirmed. 2WD is fine on the main tar roads. Fewer crowds than Kruger, same Big Five.

~2.5 hrs from Durban Big Five Self-Drive White Rhino Dawn Game Drive 2WD Accessible
Day 14Sun 9 Feb
Umhlanga & Durban Nightlife

Last Durban day. Umhlanga Rocks — iconic lighthouse, Whalebone Pier, upmarket restaurants and warm water. Sunset from the pier is beautiful. Then Florida Road for Durban's best restaurants and nightlife before the inland push tomorrow.

Umhlanga Rocks Beach Whalebone Pier Sunset Florida Road Durban Nightlife

Drakensberg & Home

Feb 10–12 · 3 Days
Royal Natal — Drakensberg
Day 15Mon 10 Feb
Durban → Royal Natal NP

Drive inland. The shift from subtropical coast to mountain is dramatic. Royal Natal NP sits at the foot of the Amphitheatre — a 5km basalt cliff wall rising 1,100m from the valley floor. One of the great natural spectacles of Africa. Afternoon hike to the base of Tugela Falls.

~3.5 hrs from Durban Royal Natal NP The Amphitheatre Tugela Falls Hike Mountain Chalet
Day 16Tue 11 Feb
Full Day in the Drakensberg

Sunrise hike to the Amphitheatre face. Then the Gorge trail — mountain pools, cold fresh air, and a scale of landscape that makes you feel appropriately small. Golden-hour light on the basalt wall is extraordinary. January means lush green valleys and dramatic cloud formations building over the plateau by afternoon.

Amphitheatre Sunrise Gorge Trail Mountain Pools Golden Hour Photography
Day 17Wed 12 Feb
Midlands Meander → Johannesburg

The perfect close. A gentle drive home through the KZN Midlands — rolling green hills, craft studios, cheese farms, fly-fishing rivers. A long farm lunch somewhere in Nottingham Road, then the N3 north. Home by evening. Cards full. Trip done.

~5 hrs total Midlands Meander Nottingham Road Farm Lunch Home — Johannesburg
